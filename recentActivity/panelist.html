RESEARCH AND CAPSTONE PROPOSAL DEFENSE PANELIST

April 24, 2026 - College of Engineering and Technology Education, Holy Trinity College
Image Source: https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=1535945961868401&set=pb.100063590364810.-2207520000&type=3

On April 24, 2026, I had the privilege of serving as a member of the evaluation panel for the Research and Capstone Proposal Defenses at the College of Engineering and Technology Education (CETE), Holy Trinity College of General Santos City. This academic forum serves as a critical gateway for Information Technology and Computer Science students as they transition from theoretical concepts into practical, software, and hardware system architectures.

As a panelist, my role involved meticulously evaluating project methodologies, architectural blueprints, data workflows, and the societal relevance of the proposed tech interventions. I engaged with various student groups to offer technical feedback, identify potential computational bottlenecks, and help refine their system integration pipelines.

Collaborating alongside with my fellow educators to uphold high institutional standards was a rewarding experience. Reviewing these proposals highlights the continuous evolution of technological education and emphasizes the importance of mentoring the next generation of IT practitioners as they design innovative solutions for real-world industries.
